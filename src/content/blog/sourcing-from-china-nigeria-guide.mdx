China remains the largest source market for Nigerian importers, supplying everything from electronics and fashion to machinery and raw materials. But with opportunity comes risk — supplier scams, quality inconsistencies, and communication gaps are real challenges. This guide walks through a systematic approach to sourcing from China that minimises those risks.

## Step 1: Finding the Right Supplier

Start with verified platforms like Alibaba (look for Gold Supplier or Verified badges), Canton Trade Fair contacts, or industry-specific sourcing agents who already have vetted supplier lists.

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Prices significantly below market average
- Pressure to pay via unofficial channels
- Limited product photos or vague specifications
- Reluctance to provide business licence or factory address

The best suppliers are willing to share their business registration, factory photos, and client references. If a supplier avoids transparency, move on.

## Step 2: Verifying Before Payment

Verification is the most important step in the sourcing process. A simple video call to see the factory floor and speak directly with the production manager can reveal a lot about whether the supplier is genuine.

## Step 3: Negotiating Terms Clearly

Chinese suppliers respond well to clear, professional communication. Specify every detail in writing: product specifications, packaging requirements, quantity, unit price, delivery terms (Incoterms), payment terms, and production timeline.

**Common payment terms:**
- **T/T (Telegraphic Transfer)** — 30% deposit, 70% before shipment
- **L/C (Letter of Credit)** — bank-guaranteed payment, safer but more complex
- **Trade Assurance** — Alibaba's escrow-style protection

## Step 4: Quality Inspection Before Shipment

Never skip inspection. A pre-shipment inspection by an independent inspector or a trusted agent confirms that the goods match the agreed specifications before they leave the factory. This is where defects, quantity shortages, or specification deviations are caught — while correction is still possible.

## Step 5: Shipping and Logistics Coordination

Once the goods pass inspection, the logistics chain begins: export packing, freight booking, customs documentation, international shipping, Nigerian customs clearance, and final delivery. Each stage requires coordination between multiple parties.
