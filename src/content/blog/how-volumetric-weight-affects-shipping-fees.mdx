category: "Buying Guides"
date: "July 2026"

excerpt: "Many customers focus only on scale weight, but in freight, package size can matter just as much."
---

Volumetric weight estimates how much space a package occupies during transport. It is commonly used when a package is light but physically large.

A practical air-cargo guide is Length × Width × Height in cm divided by 5000. If the volumetric result is higher than the actual scale weight, the volumetric figure becomes the chargeable weight.

That is why a light but oversized carton can cost more than a smaller and heavier parcel. Understanding this early helps customers choose better packaging and the right shipping mode.
