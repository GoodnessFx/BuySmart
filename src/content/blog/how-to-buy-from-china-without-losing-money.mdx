The cheapest quotation is not always the safest quotation. Before paying any supplier, confirm the business identity, product history, communication quality, and ability to deliver what was promised.

Inspection should not be treated as an optional luxury for high-value orders. A simple pre-shipment check can catch wrong colors, wrong quantity, damaged items, or poor finishing before the goods move.

Shipping mode should match the product. Air freight helps when speed matters, while sea freight helps when size and weight would make air too expensive.

Ask for a landed-cost outlook before payment. Product cost, exchange impact, procurement fee, inspection, and shipping should all be explained clearly from the beginning.
