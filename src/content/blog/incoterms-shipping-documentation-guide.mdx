One of the most common sources of confusion in international trade is who pays for what, and when. Incoterms (International Commercial Terms) are the standard trade terms that define the responsibilities of buyer and seller. Understanding them is essential for every Nigerian importer.

## What Are Incoterms?

Incoterms are a set of internationally recognised rules published by the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC). They define who arranges and pays for transportation, insurance, and customs clearance at each stage of the shipping process. The latest version is Incoterms 2020.

**The most common terms for Nigeria imports:**

**EXW (Ex Works).** The buyer is responsible for all costs and risks from the supplier factory door. The seller makes the goods available at their premises, and the buyer handles everything else — export packing, transportation to port, export customs, freight, import clearance, and final delivery.

**FOB (Free on Board).** The seller delivers the goods to the port of origin and loads them onto the vessel. Risk transfers to the buyer once the goods are on board. The buyer handles freight, insurance, and all costs from that point onward. FOB is the most commonly used term for Chinese suppliers shipping to Nigeria.

**CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight).** The seller pays for transportation and insurance to the destination port in Nigeria. Risk transfers to the buyer once the goods are loaded on the vessel, but the seller handles the logistics up to arrival at the Nigerian port.

**DDP (Delivered Duty Paid).** The seller bears all costs and risks, including delivery to the buyer's location in Nigeria and payment of import duties. This is the least common term for Nigeria imports, as foreign suppliers are often not registered to clear customs locally.

## Why Incoterms Matter

Without clearly agreed Incoterms, misunderstandings can lead to unexpected costs. For example, if you agree on EXW without realising it, you are responsible for arranging export customs clearance in China and trucking the goods from the factory to the port — costs you may not have budgeted for.

**Always confirm the Incoterm before the quote.** It affects every line item in the supplier's pricing.

## Essential Shipping Documents

Regardless of the Incoterm, every international shipment requires certain standard documents:

- **Commercial Invoice** — the supplier's bill showing the transaction value, HS code, and description
- **Packing List** — details weight, dimensions, and contents of each package
- **Bill of Lading (sea) or Airway Bill (air)** — the transport contract and title document
- **Certificate of Origin** — may reduce duty rates under preferential trade arrangements
- **Form M** — mandatory for all imports into Nigeria, processed before shipment
